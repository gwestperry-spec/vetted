How to Score a LinkedIn Job Posting with Vetted

By George West-Perry, Founder of Vetted: Career Intelligence

April 24, 2026 · Updated April 24, 2026

I didn't learn this trick until I was building Vetted. That's embarrassing to admit, but it's true — and it tells you something about how little most people know about what you can actually do with a LinkedIn job posting before you apply.

Here's the situation: LinkedIn blocks automated tools from reading job postings. Vetted can fetch job descriptions from Indeed, Greenhouse, Lever, Workday, and most company career pages — but LinkedIn specifically locks out any automated access. The entire description is behind a login wall that no tool can bypass.

What most people don't realize is that you can still score any LinkedIn job in Vetted in under 30 seconds. You just have to do one manual step first.

The Method (Pick the one that fits your situation)

Step 1

Open the LinkedIn job posting

Navigate to the job in the LinkedIn app or in your browser. You need to be signed into LinkedIn — the full description is only visible when you're logged in.

Step 2

Expand the full description

Scroll to "About the job" and tap "...more" to expand the complete text. Don't skip this — if the description is truncated, Vetted is scoring an incomplete picture.

Step 3A — iPhone or iPad

Long-press to copy

Press and hold your finger anywhere inside the job description text until a popup appears with Copy and Share… options. Tap Copy.

Do not use Select All — that grabs everything on the page, not just the job description.

Step 3B — Mac or Windows

Highlight and copy the description

Click at the start of the job description text, then drag to highlight the section you want — or hold Shift and click the end. Then:

Mac: Cmd + C to copy
Windows: Ctrl + C to copy

Avoid Cmd/Ctrl + A — that selects everything on the browser page, not just the job posting.

Step 4

Paste into Vetted and score

Open Vetted, tap the input field, paste the copied text, and hit Score. Vetted returns your VQ score and a Pursue, Monitor, or Pass verdict in under a minute — scored against your own weighted priority framework.

External apply role? There's a faster way. If the posting has an Apply button (not "Easy Apply"), it links out to the company's own career page. Copy that URL, remove any LinkedIn tracking parameters (?utm_source=linkedin and anything after it), and paste the clean URL directly into Vetted. Vetted can fetch that page automatically — no copy-paste needed.

If it's an Easy Apply role, the copy-paste method above is your only option.

Why LinkedIn Blocks Automated Access

LinkedIn restricts automated tools from reading job postings — job descriptions are part of their core business model and they actively protect that data. Vetted can fetch job descriptions automatically from most other major platforms: Indeed, Greenhouse, Lever, Workday, and direct company career pages. LinkedIn is the specific exception.

The manual method takes about 30 seconds. The score is just as accurate. The verdict is just as sharp. You're doing one extra step that LinkedIn forces on you — that's it.

Most people spend 20–40 minutes reading and re-reading a LinkedIn job description trying to decide if it's worth pursuing. Thirty seconds to copy-paste it into a framework that returns a systematic verdict is still an enormous time save.

What Vetted Does With It

Once you paste the job description, Vetted evaluates it across five filters — Financial Accountability, Access to Leadership, Clear Success Measures, Organizational Impact, and Role Integrity — each weighted to your own career priorities. The result is a VQ score between 1 and 5 and a clear Pursue, Monitor, or Pass verdict.

The scoring takes under a minute. The verdict tells you whether to invest time in this opportunity before you've spent a single hour preparing an application or researching the company.
